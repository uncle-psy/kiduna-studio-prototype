## Purpose

This is a **UX prototype refinement backlog**, not a production engineering plan. Each item should become a believable screen, interaction, or scenario that helps us find gaps in the Studio model.

## P0 — broaden the domain model in the prototype

- [ ] Show an organization containing multiple communities, with inherited and locally overridden Stance.
- [ ] Add a second community and two simultaneous projects so Field density, orientation, and switching can be evaluated.
- [ ] Create focused views for an Envoy's Wisdom, Stance, Connections, Skills, and Automations.
- [ ] Let members inspect and adjust what their Ally can share with a community or project Envoy.
- [ ] Make invitation states believable for all three identities: proposed, sent, viewed, accepted, declined, and expired.
- [ ] Show private, community, project, organization, and public Wisdom boundaries with provenance.
- [ ] Add artifact/source inspection: origin, contributor, version, citations, and effective access.
- [ ] Give Mapper invocation a clear purpose, scope preview, progress, pause/cancel, completion, and reusable output.
- [ ] Prototype small contextual grant requests and receipts wherever an Ally, Envoy, or Actor crosses a boundary.

## P1 — work and coordination use cases

- [ ] David asks Lumen to share a thought urgently with Jeya; Mira decides how and when to interrupt her.
- [ ] Jeya asks Mira what changed in the project since yesterday, with cited Wisdom and receipts.
- [ ] Aashik brings an external Telegram exchange into project Wisdom without exposing the private conversation.
- [ ] The community Envoy receives a Slack signal, associates it with the right project, and requests confirmation before sharing.
- [ ] Two projects use the same source with different scopes and reach different synthesized directions.
- [ ] A member disagrees with a proposed direction and asks their Ally to negotiate a revision through the Envoys.
- [ ] A project pauses, forks, completes, and becomes durable community Wisdom.
- [ ] A member leaves a community while their prior contributions retain understandable provenance and policy.
- [ ] A member moves between spatial Field, compact list, and keyboard-only modes without losing context.

## P2 — visual and interaction exploration

- [ ] Stress-test the living color system for ten-plus entities, status, urgency, and accessibility.
- [ ] Refine Faces across people, Allies, Envoys, and Actors without falling back to initials or profile-photo conventions.
- [ ] Test when the HUD should be translucent, nearly opaque, split, or minimized.
- [ ] Add calm ambient signals for presence, new Wisdom, blocked work, and required attention.
- [ ] Prototype creation flows that begin in natural language but reveal editable structure before commitment.
- [ ] Add lightweight first-use guidance that teaches the Field through action rather than a tour.

## Questions each iteration should answer

1. Can someone tell whether they are seeing a person, Ally, Envoy, Actor, container, or piece of Wisdom without reading a label?
2. Does the Field remain useful as entity count and activity increase?
3. Which boundaries require explicit consent, and which can be governed by standing Stance?
4. How do Allies coordinate meaningful work without recreating a group chat?
5. How much of an Envoy's internal structure should be visible during ordinary work?
6. Can every important recommendation be traced to Wisdom, scope, and a retained receipt?
7. What must remain visible when focused work makes the HUD nearly opaque?
